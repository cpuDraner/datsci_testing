# Entity Resolution
- Entity Resolution is a use of machine learning that links together records that may be written ambiguously. It can also be refered to as the 'phonebook problem', as a good example of using record linkage is finding typos between two sets of names across two different phonebooks. 
- Week 1: This week we went over the basics of coding in a group environment. Since entity resolution is an application of coding practices, this is somewhat directly related.
- Week 2: This week we (re)learned about pandas, working directly with Grants and Articles dataframes. This basic data processing is vital to entity resolution.
- Week 3: This week focused on scikit learn and classifiers. Classifiers are necessary to tell our machine how to group records, thus being a necessary part of entity resolution.
- Week 4: For week 4 we worked with natural language processing. Natural language processing is an integral part of entity resolution. It allows the computer to parse and compare the text we need to link.
- Week 5: For week 5 we designed a program to produce training data for the direct purpose of entity resolution. 
- Week 6: For week 6, we actually made the entity resolution git. This is very obviously related to entity resolution.
- Week 7: For week 7, we began working with databases. Having database integrations can be helpful when working with particularly large amounts of data, and thus can be integrated into our record linkage programs.
